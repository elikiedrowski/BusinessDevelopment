# Company Name Candidates

Initial brainstorm for the venture's brand. None are committed — these are starting points for the next meeting discussion.

## Criteria

- Evocative and ownable; not generic
- Single word preferred
- Avoids "claim," "insurance," or "AI" in the name (TurboTax doesn't put "tax" first; the brand carries the category)
- Works in both consumer ad copy and B2B conversations with attorneys / contractors
- Doesn't imply representation, legal advice, or PA services (regulatory positioning constraint)

## Top 5

### 1. Counterclaim

Direct legal force. Mirrors the "adversarial relationship with your carrier" framing exactly — the homeowner finally pushing back. Punchy, ownable, sharp. The slight aggression is a feature: this is a tool for fighting underpayment, not for filing paperwork.

- **Strengths:** Clear positioning, memorable, legal weight
- **Risks:** May sound too litigation-heavy for a software-tool brand; need to ensure it doesn't undermine the "not representation" positioning

### 2. Fulcrum

The leverage point. Conceptually precise — the platform is the leverage homeowners gain over a $2T industry. Sounds enterprise-grade, which helps when Paymon's pitching law firms to buy leads.

- **Strengths:** Sophisticated, works both consumer and B2B, less aggressive than Counterclaim
- **Risks:** Less obvious meaning; "Fulcrum" is used by several existing financial/consulting firms — domain availability uncertain

### 3. Vantage

The homeowner's first real vantage point against the carrier — visibility, position, upper hand. Clean, modern, single word. Works equally in consumer ad copy ("get your Vantage") and B2B conversations.

- **Strengths:** Easy to say, easy to remember, positive framing
- **Risks:** Used by several existing brands (Vantage Point, Vantage Bank, etc.); may need a modifier

### 4. Reckon

Bold, plain-spoken, slight Southern register that lands in our highest-volume disaster geographies (FL, TX, LA). "Time to reckon with your insurance company." Personality the others don't have, and "reckon" carries both *to negotiate* and *to bring to account* — both halves of the product.

- **Strengths:** Distinctive voice, regional resonance, double meaning
- **Risks:** Slang register may not translate to B2B partner pitches; some buyers may find it too casual

### 5. Aftermath

Owns the post-disaster moment exactly. Emotionally resonant — this is when the customer finds you. Memorable URL and a clear marketing wedge: *"Aftermath is what happens after the storm."*

- **Strengths:** Emotionally precise, ownable, marketing copy nearly writes itself
- **Risks:** Negative connotation may turn off some users; doesn't communicate what the product does

## Honorable mentions

- **Bulwark** — Protective, defensive, strong. Good if we want to lean into "we're your defense."
- **Underwrite** — Flips the carrier's own word back ("underwrite yourself"). Clever but risks confusing users.
- **Tilt** — Short, evocative of scales tilting toward you, slightly playful.
- **Polaris** — North-star guidance through the storm. Solid but a touch startup-cliché.

## Eli's current pick

**Counterclaim.** Most ownable, hardest to confuse with anything else, and the name itself does sales work in ad copy.
