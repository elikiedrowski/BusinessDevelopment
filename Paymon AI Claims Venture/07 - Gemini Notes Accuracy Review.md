# Gemini Notes Accuracy Review

Gemini's notes are directionally useful and capture the major meeting outcomes, but they flatten several important nuances. Use them as a meeting index, not as the final strategic record.

## Overall grade

**B / B+ for recall; C+ for strategic nuance.**

The summary correctly identifies:
- A disruptive AI-assisted insurance claim platform
- B2B lead generation as the preferred monetization path
- Technical feasibility as the next phase
- Naggie's role / compensation as an unresolved topic
- The phased roadmap: feasibility, business structuring, launch roles

## What Gemini got right

- The team aligned on exploring a real partnership, not just brainstorming.
- The consumer product should be free or low-cost and should help homeowners handle their own claims.
- Revenue should come primarily from high-intent B2B leads, especially attorneys and contractors.
- Naggie is expected to be technically important and needs meaningful incentive alignment.
- The group agreed to keep a regular cadence and schedule the next meeting late the following week.

## What Gemini underplayed

- **The key strategic distinction from Bo/Noble:** Bo's "Turboax" appears to extend traditional public adjusting. Paymon's idea is to replace the PA middleman for ordinary consumers.
- **The regulatory positioning is central:** The product must be software that helps users represent themselves, not a public-adjusting service that represents them.
- **The claim documentation / estimate problem is a major product dependency:** Paymon emphasized Matterport, estimates, photos, proof of loss, and the need for stronger evidence.
- **Paymon's playbook is the real asset:** The LLM is not the moat by itself. The moat is Paymon's claim workflow knowledge encoded into guided steps and correspondence.
- **The biggest technical question is not "can we make an app?"** It is whether AI can extract policy obligations, exclusions, limits, and claim procedures accurately with citations.

## What Gemini may overstate

- **"Led by their technical architect"** — The discussion positioned Naggie as the lead technical brain, but the feasibility work is really Eli + Naggie under the CRM Wizards umbrella.
- **"Professional firms" as a generic revenue source** — The real B2B buyers discussed were more specific: attorneys, roofers, HVAC, drywall / contractors, and possibly PAs as fallback.
- **"Business model partnership established"** — The partnership intent is aligned, but the structure is not established. Equity, investment, IP, Naggie's role, and decision rights are still open.

## Corrections to preserve

- Treat "deny, delay, defend" and the 10% initial-offer claim as Paymon's framing until externally verified.
- Treat "Paymon's law firm relationships will buy leads" as a strong hypothesis, not validated revenue.
- Treat the software-tool regulatory model as a thesis that needs research, not a settled answer.
